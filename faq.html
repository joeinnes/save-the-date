FAQs

Can I bring my children?

Yes! Children are welcome at the wedding, and will be listed on your invitation. Be aware that although Budapest is very safe, the area immediately outside the venue is publicly accessible, so children should be supervised. We are planning to help with this for at least some of the event.

We would also ask that children stay quiet during the ceremony—otherwise we'd ask you to pop outside with them until they've calmed down.

Can I take photos?

We'd love you to take photos! After the wedding, we'll share a link where you can share your best pictures with us and the other wedding-goers. We'd rather you didn't upload any photos to social media.

We would however ask you to avoid taking photos during the ceremony itself—we've paid for professional photographers, and we'd rather they didn't miss the shot because someone else's camera got in the way!

Where can I stay?

We'll be sharing accommodation suggestions shortly.

Will my Uber work?

Unfortunately, Uber has been chased out of Hungary, but there is a similar app called Bolt. It's fast, relatively cheap, and works pretty well.

What drinks will be available?

Soft drinks (fizzy, mineral water, coffee, fruit juice) will be available all night. There will also be an open bar with local Hungarian beers (unfiltered light beer, dark beer) and alcohol-free Heineken, as well as a selection of high-quality Hungarian wines.

Hungary has a fantastic climate and soil for growing wine, and the quality and standard of Hungarian wines is very good.

Do I need a car?

Almost certainly not. The event itself is easy to get to by walking (approx 10-15 minutes from nearest public transport stops), or by taxi. Budapest itself is a very good value place to stay, and the public transport system is very effective and fast.

What should I see around the city?

There are loads of cool things to see and do around the city. We'd recommend going up Gellert Hill, taking a trip to the Castle district, visiting the infamous Szimpla kert. The Parliament building is also very impressive If you find yourself with a bit of time on your hands, pop down to Margaret Island, and wander around the gardens a bit.
